THE PROBLEM
Why this exists

If you ski, climb, or hike anything technical, you already know the data problem. Conditions reports live in scattered Reddit threads, fragmented Facebook groups, blog posts six months out of date, or word-of-mouth at the trailhead. There's no single place where the people actually doing these activities share what they saw, today, on this route.

Existing fitness apps treat the outdoors like a treadmill — they care about distance, pace, calories. Existing social platforms aren't built around routes, summits, and the specific information backcountry users need. There's a real product gap, and I've felt it personally on every multi-day trip.

THE APPROACH
How it works

Native iOS application built in Swift. Authentication layer supports Sign in with Apple, Sign in with Google, and standard email/password — all with persistent login state, so users stay signed in across sessions. Backend on Firebase for user data, posts, route information, and conditions reporting.

Core features in development: route tracking with summit logging, condition reports tied to specific peaks and dates, photo carousel posts, and a feed organized around your followed routes. Long-term roadmap includes avalanche forecast integration, gear lists, and trip planning tools. Current scope: get the social fundamentals right first, then expand.
